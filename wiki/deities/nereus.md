## Overview

**Nereus** is the eldest child of [Pontus](pontus.md) (Sea), known as "the Old Man" for his trustworthy and righteous nature.

## Personality

"Men call him the Old Man because he is trusty and gentle and does not forget the laws of righteousness, but thinks just and kindly thoughts."[^theog-old] He "is true and lies not."[^theog-true]

[^theog-old]: "men call him the Old Man because he is trusty and gentle and does not forget the laws of righteousness, but thinks just and kindly thoughts" (Theogony 234-236, trans. H.G. Evelyn-White)
[^theog-true]: "Sea begat Nereus, the eldest of his children, who is true and lies not" (Theogony 233)

## Children

With Doris, "daughter of Ocean the perfect river," Nereus fathered fifty daughters (the Nereids), described as "passing lovely amongst goddesses" and "skilled in excellent crafts."[^theog-nereids] Among them: Amphitrite, Thetis, Galatea, and Psamathe.

[^theog-nereids]: "of Nereus and rich-haired Doris, daughter of Ocean the perfect river, were born children, passing lovely amongst goddesses... These fifty daughters sprang from blameless Nereus, skilled in excellent crafts" (Theogony 240-264)
