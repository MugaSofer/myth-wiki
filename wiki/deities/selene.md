## Overview

**Selene** is the goddess of the moon, daughter of [Hyperion](hyperion.md) and [Theia](theia.md), sister of [Helius](helius.md) and [Eos](eos.md).

## Epithets

- **"clear Selene"** — ([Theogony](../sources/theogony.md) 371)
- **"bright Selene"** — (Hymn XXXII 8)
- **"rich-tressed Selene"** — (Hymn XXXI 4)
- **"white-armed goddess, bright Selene, mild, bright-tressed queen"** — the closing salutation (Hymn XXXII 17)[^desc-queen]

[^desc-queen]: "Hail, white-armed goddess, bright Selene, mild, bright-tressed queen!" (Homeric Hymn XXXII to Selene 17-18, trans. H.G. Evelyn-White)

## Appearance

[Homeric Hymn XXXII](../sources/homeric-hymns.md) gives a luminous physical description: "From her immortal head a radiance is shown from heaven and embraces earth; and great is the beauty that ariseth from her shining light. The air, unlit before, glows with the light of her golden crown." She drives "long-maned horses at full speed" after "having bathed her lovely body in the waters of Ocean."[^desc-appearance]

[^desc-appearance]: "From her immortal head a radiance is shown from heaven and embraces earth; and great is the beauty that ariseth from her shining light. The air, unlit before, glows with the light of her golden crown, and her rays beam clear, whensoever bright Selene having bathed her lovely body in the waters of Ocean, and donned her far-gleaming, shining team, drives on her long-maned horses at full speed" (Homeric Hymn XXXII to Selene 1-10)

## Birth

"[Theia](theia.md) was subject in love to [Hyperion](hyperion.md) and bare great [Helius](helius.md) (Sun) and clear Selene (Moon) and [Eos](eos.md) (Dawn) who shines upon all that are on earth and upon the deathless Gods who live in the wide heaven."[^theog-birth]

[^theog-birth]: "Theia was subject in love to Hyperion and bare great Helius (Sun) and clear Selene (Moon) and Eos (Dawn) who shines upon all that are on earth and upon the deathless Gods who live in the wide heaven" (Theogony 371-374, trans. H.G. Evelyn-White)

## Variant Traditions

### Mother's Name

The [Theogony](../sources/theogony.md) names Selene's mother as [Theia](theia.md). [Homeric Hymn XXXI](../sources/homeric-hymns.md) (to [Helius](helius.md)) gives the alternate name Euryphaessa ("the far-shining one"): "[Hyperion](hyperion.md) wedded glorious Euryphaessa, his own sister, who bare him lovely children, rosy-armed [Eos](eos.md) and rich-tressed Selene and tireless [Helius](helius.md)."[^hh31-mother]

[^hh31-mother]: "Hyperion wedded glorious Euryphaessa, his own sister, who bare him lovely children, rosy-armed Eos and rich-tressed Selene and tireless Helios who is like the deathless gods" (Homeric Hymn XXXI to Helios 2-5, trans. H.G. Evelyn-White)

## Children

"Once the Son of [Cronos](cronos.md) [[Zeus](zeus.md)] was joined with her in love; and she conceived and bare a daughter Pandia, exceeding lovely amongst the deathless gods."[^hh32-pandia]

[^hh32-pandia]: "Once the Son of Cronos was joined with her in love; and she conceived and bare a daughter Pandia, exceeding lovely amongst the deathless gods" (Homeric Hymn XXXII to Selene 14-16, trans. H.G. Evelyn-White)
