## Overview

The **Graces** (Charites) are three goddesses of beauty and charm.

## Birth

"Eurynome, the daughter of [Ocean](oceanus.md), beautiful in form, bare him [[Zeus](zeus.md)] three fair-cheeked Charites (Graces), Aglaea, and Euphrosyne, and lovely Thaleia, from whose eyes as they glanced flowed love that unnerves the limbs: and beautiful is their glance beneath their brows."[^theog-birth]

[^theog-birth]: "Eurynome, the daughter of Ocean, beautiful in form, bare him three fair-cheeked Charites (Graces), Aglaea, and Euphrosyne, and lovely Thaleia, from whose eyes as they glanced flowed love that unnerves the limbs: and beautiful is their glance beneath their brows" (Theogony 907-911, trans. H.G. Evelyn-White)

## Members

- **Aglaea** — the youngest; married [Hephaestus](hephaestus.md)[^theog-aglaea]
- **Euphrosyne**
- **Thaleia**

[^theog-aglaea]: "Hephaestus, the famous Lame One, made Aglaea, youngest of the Graces, his buxom wife" (Theogony 945-946, trans. H.G. Evelyn-White)

## Appearances

- **[Theogony](../sources/theogony.md)** 64, 907-911, 945-946: Mentioned near the Muses' homes; birth; Aglaea's marriage to Hephaestus
- **[Bibliotheca](../sources/apollodorus.md)** 1.3.1: "by Eurynome, daughter of Ocean, he had the Graces, to wit, Aglaia, Euphrosyne, and Thalia" — confirms Theogony
