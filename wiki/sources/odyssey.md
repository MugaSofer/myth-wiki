|---|---|

## Overview

The **Odyssey** is an epic poem attributed to Homer, set in the years following the fall of Troy. It follows Odysseus's long journey home to Ithaca and his son Telemachus's coming of age. The gods play a guiding role throughout — [Athena](../deities/athena.md) as Odysseus's patron, [Poseidon](../deities/poseidon.md) as his antagonist, and [Zeus](../deities/zeus.md) as the ultimate arbiter.

## Books Processed

- **Book I**: Council of the gods; [Zeus](../deities/zeus.md)'s speech on mortal responsibility (Aegisthus); [Athena](../deities/athena.md) advocates for Odysseus; [Poseidon](../deities/poseidon.md)'s wrath over [Polyphemus](../creatures/cyclopes.md); [Atlas](../deities/atlas.md) as pillar-bearer and father of Calypso; Athena disguised as Mentes visits Telemachus; [Hermes](../deities/hermes.md) as past messenger to Aegisthus; Athena departs "like an eagle of the sea"
- **Book II**: The assembly at Ithaca; [Zeus](../deities/zeus.md) sends two eagles as omen; [Themis](../deities/themis.md) "who looseth and gathereth the meetings of men"; [Athena](../deities/athena.md) disguised as Mentor and as Telemachus; sends a favourable West Wind for the voyage to Pylos
- **Book III**: At Pylos; sacrifice to [Poseidon](../deities/poseidon.md); [Athena](../deities/athena.md) as Mentor guides Telemachus; "death, which is common to all, the very gods cannot avert"; Athena departs as sea-eagle; Nestor's tale of the Greek returns; [Apollo](../deities/apollo.md) slays Menelaus's pilot "with the visitation of his gentle shafts"
- **Book V**: Second divine council; [Zeus](../deities/zeus.md) commands [Hermes](../deities/hermes.md) to Calypso; Hermes's golden sandals, wand, cormorant flight; Calypso's cave described; Calypso's speech on divine jealousy — [Eos](../deities/eos.md) loved Orion ([Artemis](../deities/artemis.md) slew him), [Demeter](../deities/demeter.md) lay with Iasion (Zeus slew him); the Styx oath ("greatest oath and most terrible"); [Poseidon](../deities/poseidon.md) returns from Ethiopia and unleashes a storm; Ino/Leucothea gives Odysseus her imperishable veil; [Athena](../deities/athena.md) binds the winds and guides Odysseus to Phaeacia; the river-god stays his stream; Athena sheds sleep on Odysseus
- **Book VI**: [Athena](../deities/athena.md) disguised as Dymas's daughter sends Nausicaa to the river; the description of [Olympus](../places/olympus.md) ("the seat of the gods that standeth fast for ever"); Nausicaa compared to [Artemis](../deities/artemis.md); Athena gives Nausicaa courage; Athena beautifies Odysseus (hyacinth curls, gold-on-silver simile); Odysseus compares Nausicaa to Artemis and mentions [Apollo](../deities/apollo.md)'s altar at Delos; Athena "had regard unto her father's brother [Poseidon]"
- **Book VII**: [Athena](../deities/athena.md) as young maiden guides Odysseus through mist; Phaeacian genealogy ([Poseidon](../deities/poseidon.md) + Periboea, daughter of Eurymedon king of [Giants](../creatures/giants.md)); [Apollo](../deities/apollo.md) slew Rhexenor; [Hephaestus](../deities/hephaestus.md)'s golden and silver hounds ("free from death and age"); Athena "hath given them notable wisdom in all fair handiwork"; Athena departs to "the good house of Erechtheus" in Athens; Fate and "the stern spinning women"; Rhadamanthus visits Tityos son of Gaia; gods "appear manifest" to Phaeacians — "near of kin to them, even as are the Cyclôpes and the wild tribes of the Giants"
- **Book VIII**: [Athena](../deities/athena.md) as herald, pours grace on Odysseus; the [Muse](../deities/groups/muses.md) gave Demodocus sight and song ("of his sight she reft him, but granted him sweet song"); [Apollo](../deities/apollo.md)'s oracle at Pytho; **The Song of [Ares](../deities/ares.md) and [Aphrodite](../deities/aphrodite.md)** — adultery in [Hephaestus](../deities/hephaestus.md)'s house, [Helius](../deities/helius.md) reports it, Hephaestus forges an unbreakable net, gods witness ([Poseidon](../deities/poseidon.md), [Hermes](../deities/hermes.md), Apollo), Poseidon guarantees the fine, Aphrodite goes to Paphos where the [Graces](../deities/groups/graces.md) bathe her; Athena marks discus throw; Apollo slew Eurytus; the Wooden Horse song (Epeius made it by aid of Athena); Poseidon's prophecy against Phaeacian ships; "the gods have fashioned, and have woven the skein of death for men, that there might be a song in the ears even of the folk of aftertime"
- **Book XI**: The Nekuia — descent to Hades; Elpenor's shade requests burial; Teiresias's prophecy (golden sceptre; [Poseidon](../deities/poseidon.md)'s wrath; cattle of [Helius](../deities/helius.md) on Thrinacia; return to Ithaca; slaying the wooers; the oar-journey inland; sacrifice to Poseidon; gentle death "from the sea"); Anticleia on the nature of death ("[Persephone](../deities/persephone.md), the daughter of [Zeus](../deities/zeus.md), doth in no wise deceive thee"); **Catalogue of Heroines** — Tyro seduced by [Poseidon](../deities/poseidon.md) as river Enipeus (bore Pelias and Neleus); Antiope bore Amphion and Zethus to [Zeus](../deities/zeus.md); Alcmene bore [Heracles](../heroes/heracles.md) to Zeus; Epicaste/Oedipus ("the Avengers of a mother" — [Erinyes](../creatures/erinyes.md)); Leda, Castor and Polydeuces ("living one day and dying the next"); Iphimedeia bore Otos and Ephialtes to Poseidon (piled mountains, slain by [Apollo](../deities/apollo.md)); [Artemis](../deities/artemis.md) slew Ariadne "by reason of the witness of [Dionysus](../deities/dionysus.md)"; Agamemnon's shade warns Odysseus; Achilles: "Rather would I live on ground as the hireling of another... than bear sway among all the dead"; [Athena](../deities/athena.md) and the judgment of Aias's arms; Minos judging the dead ("glorious son of Zeus, wielding a golden sceptre"); Orion hunting; Tityos punished ("son of renowned Earth... dealt violently with Leto"); Tantalus; Sisyphus; [Heracles](../heroes/heracles.md)' phantom ("as for himself he hath joy at the banquet among the deathless gods... Hebe of the fair ankles"); Heracles recalls fetching Cerberus with aid of [Hermes](../deities/hermes.md) and Athena; [Persephone](../deities/persephone.md) controls shades, threatens with Gorgon head
- **Book XII**: [Circe](../deities/circe.md)'s prophecies (Sirens, Scylla, Charybdis, cattle of [Helius](../deities/helius.md)); the Sirens' song ("we know all things"); Scylla described (twelve feet, six necks, "an immortal plague"); [Hera](../deities/hera.md) saved the Argo past the Wandering Rocks "for love of Jason"; six men snatched by Scylla; Thrinacia — Lampetie reports cattle slaughter to [Helius](../deities/helius.md); Helius threatens to "shine among the dead"; [Zeus](../deities/zeus.md) destroys the ship with thunderbolt; Odysseus survives on wreckage past Charybdis to Ogygia
- **Book XIII**: [Poseidon](../deities/poseidon.md) complains to [Zeus](../deities/zeus.md) about the Phaeacians; Zeus authorises punishment; Poseidon smites the Phaeacian ship into stone; haven of Phorcys "the ancient one of the sea"; cave of the Naiads; [Athena](../deities/athena.md) disguised as young herdsman; Athena reveals herself ("both of us practised in deceits"); explains not opposing Poseidon "my own father's brother"; transforms Odysseus to beggar with her wand
- **Book XIV**: Light on mythology; [Zeus](../deities/zeus.md) as god of strangers ("from Zeus are all strangers and beggars"); Dodona oracle ("the high leafy oak tree of the god"); [Hermes](../deities/hermes.md) "gives grace and glory to all men's work"; sacrifice portion for "the nymphs and for Hermes son of Maia"; [Artemis](../deities/artemis.md) smites the Phoenician woman
- **Book XV**: [Athena](../deities/athena.md) rouses Telemachus at Sparta; sends favourable breeze; [Hephaestus](../deities/hephaestus.md)'s silver mixing bowl; Helen prophesies via eagle omen; Melampus genealogy — "the Erinnys of the dolorous stroke" laid blindness on him; [Apollo](../deities/apollo.md) and [Zeus](../deities/zeus.md) loved Amphiaraus; [Eos](../deities/eos.md) snatched Cleitus for beauty; Apollo made Polypheides chief seer; Apollo and [Artemis](../deities/artemis.md) give gentle death on Syria; hawk as "swift messenger of Apollo"
- **Book XVI**: [Athena](../deities/athena.md) appears to Odysseus alone ("the gods in no wise appear visibly to all"); transforms Odysseus with golden wand (restores youth); later transforms him back to old man; Athena and Zeus as champions; Athena casts sleep on Penelope
- **Book X**: Aeolus, son of Hippotas — "him the son of Cronos made keeper of the winds"; floating island with bronze wall; sends Odysseus off with bag of winds; the Laestrygonians — "not like men but like the [Giants](../creatures/giants.md)"; wife "huge of bulk as a mountain peak"; [Circe](../deities/circe.md) of the braided tresses — "an awful goddess of mortal speech, own sister to the wizard Aeetes. Both were begotten of [Helios](../deities/helius.md)... and their mother was Perse, daughter of [Oceanus](../deities/oceanus.md)"; bewitched wolves and lions; turns men to swine with wand and drugs ("their mind abode even as of old"); [Hermes](../deities/hermes.md) appears "in the likeness of a young man" and gives Odysseus the herb moly ("black at the root, but the flower was like to milk... hard for mortal men to dig; howbeit with the gods all things are possible"); Circe recognises Odysseus (Hermes had told her); restores the men ("younger than before... goodlier far, and taller"); her handmaids "born of the wells and of the woods and of the holy rivers"; a year on the island; Circe's instructions for the Nekuia — "reach the dwelling of [Hades](../deities/hades.md) and of dread [Persephone](../deities/persephone.md)... the spirit of Theban Teiresias, the blind soothsayer... to him Persephone hath given judgment, even in death"; underworld geography (Acheron, Pyriphlegethon, Cocytus "a branch of the water of the [Styx](../deities/styx.md)")
- **Book IX**: Odysseus narrates; the Cicones; the Lotus-eaters; the [Cyclopes](../creatures/cyclopes.md) described as "a froward and a lawless folk" with no law or assemblies; [Polyphemus](../creatures/cyclopes.md) — "monstrous... like a wooded peak of the towering hills"; defies [Zeus](../deities/zeus.md) ("we are better men than they"); Maron "priest of [Apollo](../deities/apollo.md), the god that watched over Ismarus"; blinding with olive stake; "Noman" trick; prayer to [Poseidon](../deities/poseidon.md) — "god of the dark hair... thou avowest thee my sire"; Poseidon "heard him"; soothsayer Telemus had foretold the blinding; Zeus ignores Odysseus's sacrifice ("was devising how my decked ships and my dear company might perish"); [Athena](../deities/athena.md) invoked briefly ("Athene grant me renown")
- **Book XVII**: Odysseus at the palace as beggar; [Athena](../deities/athena.md) sheds grace on Telemachus; "the gods, in the likeness of strangers from far countries, put on all manner of shapes, and wander through the cities"; [Zeus](../deities/zeus.md) takes half a man's virtue when the day of slavery comes upon him
- **Book XVIII**: Contest with Irus; [Athena](../deities/athena.md) beautifies Penelope — "beauty imperishable, such as that wherewith the crowned Cytherea is anointed"; Penelope prays to [Artemis](../deities/artemis.md) for death
- **Book XIX**: The foot-washing; scar of the boar; Autolycus — "outdid all men in thievery and skill in swearing. This skill was the gift of the god himself, even [Hermes](../deities/hermes.md)"; [Athena](../deities/athena.md) bears a golden cresset; gates of horn and ivory for dreams
- **Book XX**: Penelope's prayer to [Artemis](../deities/artemis.md); myth of the **daughters of Pandareus** ([Aphrodite](../deities/aphrodite.md) cherished them, [Hera](../deities/hera.md) gave beauty and wisdom, [Artemis](../deities/artemis.md) gave stature, [Athena](../deities/athena.md) taught handiwork, but the storm spirits gave them to "the hateful [Erinyes](../creatures/erinyes.md)"); [Zeus](../deities/zeus.md) thunders as omen
- **Book XXI**: The bow of Eurytus; [Heracles](../heroes/heracles.md) slew Iphitus as guest ("child of Zeus, hardy of heart... had no regard for the vengeance of the gods, nor for the table which he spread before him"); [Apollo](../deities/apollo.md)'s feast day; Eurytion the Centaur at the Lapiths' feast; [Zeus](../deities/zeus.md) thunders when Odysseus strings the bow
- **Book XXII**: The slaying of the wooers; [Athena](../deities/athena.md) as Mentor chides Odysseus, then flies to roof "in fashion as a swallow"; deflects the wooers' spears; holds up her "destroying [aegis](../objects/aegis.md)"; purification with sulphur
- **Book XXIII**: Penelope's test of the bed; [Athena](../deities/athena.md) beautifies Odysseus again (hyacinth curls, gold-on-silver simile with [Hephaestus](../deities/hephaestus.md)); Athena holds night in the West and stays golden-throned Dawn by [Oceanus](../deities/oceanus.md) ("the swift-footed steeds that bear light to men, Lampus and Phaethon"); Teiresias's prophecy retold
- **Book XXIV**: **Second Nekuia** — [Hermes](../deities/hermes.md) as psychopomp leads the wooers' souls ("Past the streams of [Oceanus](../deities/oceanus.md) and the White Rock, past the gates of the Sun... the mead of asphodel"); Agamemnon's shade tells of Achilles' funeral ([Thetis](../deities/thetis.md) and the Nereids, the nine [Muses](../deities/groups/muses.md)' dirge, [Dionysus](../deities/dionysus.md)'s golden urn wrought by [Hephaestus](../deities/hephaestus.md)); Odysseus and Laertes; [Athena](../deities/athena.md) makes Laertes greater; final divine council — [Zeus](../deities/zeus.md) decrees peace and oblivion; Athena as Mentor sets covenant; [Zeus](../deities/zeus.md) casts flaming bolt
- **Book IV**: At Sparta; Helen's drug from Egypt; Helen recalls [Aphrodite](../deities/aphrodite.md)'s "blindness"; Menelaus tells of Proteus — "the deathless Egyptian Proteus, who knows the depths of every sea, and is the thrall of [Poseidon](../deities/poseidon.md)"; Proteus shape-shifts (lion, snake, pard, boar, water, tree); [Poseidon](../deities/poseidon.md) destroys Locrian Aias with his trident; [Hera](../deities/hera.md) saves Agamemnon from the sea; the **Elysian Plain** prophesied for Menelaus ("where life is easiest for men... for thou hast Helen to wife"); [Athena](../deities/athena.md) creates a phantom to comfort Penelope in her sleep
