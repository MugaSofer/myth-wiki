## Overview

The **Metamorphoses** is a Latin narrative poem in fifteen books covering myths from the creation of the cosmos to the deification of Julius Caesar, unified by the theme of transformation. It is the single most comprehensive ancient mythological text, encompassing over 250 distinct myths organized in a loosely chronological framework from primordial chaos to contemporary Rome.

Ovid draws primarily on Greek sources but writes for a Roman audience, using Latin divine names throughout. His versions often diverge from earlier Greek traditions â€” adding psychological depth, ironic detachment, and rhetorical elaboration that make his retellings distinctive. Many myths survive only through Ovid's telling (Narcissus, Pyramus and Thisbe, Pygmalion, Philemon and Baucis, etc.).

## Structure

- **Book I**: Creation, Four Ages, Lycaon, Great Flood, Daphne, Io
- **Book II**: Phaethon, Callisto, Coronis, Ocyroe, Battus, Aglauros, Europa
- **Book III**: Cadmus, Actaeon, Semele, Tiresias, Narcissus and Echo, Pentheus
- **Book IV**: Pyramus and Thisbe, Mars and Venus, Leucothoe, Hermaphroditus, Athamas and Ino, Perseus
- **Book V**: Perseus's wedding battle, Muses vs Pierides, Rape of Proserpina, Arethusa
- **Book VI**: Arachne, Niobe, Marsyas, Pelops, Tereus and Philomela, Boreas and Orithyia
- **Book VII**: Jason and Medea, Rejuvenation of Aeson, Medea and Pelias, Myrmidons, Cephalus and Procris
- **Book VIII**: Scylla of Megara, Minotaur, Daedalus and Icarus, Meleager, Baucis and Philemon, Erysichthon
- **Book IX**: Heracles and Achelous, Nessus, Death of Heracles, Byblis, Iphis
- **Book X**: Orpheus and Eurydice, Cyparissus, Ganymede, Hyacinthus, Pygmalion, Myrrha, Adonis
- **Book XI**: Death of Orpheus, Midas, Pan vs Apollo, Peleus and Thetis, Ceyx and Alcyone
- **Book XII**: Trojan War begins, Cygnus, Caeneus, Battle of Lapiths and Centaurs, Death of Achilles
- **Book XIII**: Ajax vs Ulysses, Hecuba, Memnon, Galatea and Polyphemus, Glaucus
- **Book XIV**: Scylla, Sibyl, Circe, Picus, Aeneas in Italy, Vertumnus and Pomona
- **Book XV**: Pythagoras, Aesculapius, Caesar's apotheosis
